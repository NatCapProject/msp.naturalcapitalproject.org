Step 2: Data (Compile Information)

  Overview

Since MSP is often multidisciplinary in nature, there can be substantial spatial and non-spatial data requirements. For coastal and marine spatial planning, common data requirements include biophysical and oceanographic information, such as elevation, maps of human activities and socioeconomic characteristics in the coastal zone, and data on the distribution and quality of coastal and marine habitats. When valuation is a part of the assessment, economic data is also needed, which can include market prices, property values, and discount rates. An ecosystem service approach to MSP can help to focus on the specific data requirements to minimize conflicts and maximizing the benefits people obtain from healthy ecosystems.


 

  Goals

  • Gather coarse spatial information that can be refined and improved through iterative analysis and knowledge exchange
  • Compile and curate a "working" database to use as inputs to MSP decision-support tools
  • Finalize a shareble database at the conclusion of the project
 

  Tips

  • Don’t spend too much time initially on this step
  • Use global or regional data sources to get a 'lay of the land' and identify data gaps/needs
  • Show preliminary datasets to stakeholders; use expert feedback to refine information (see Iterate step)
  • If funding permits, consider adding a dedicated GIS/data analyst to your team

      Helper Tools


    Pre-processing spatial and tabular data is a challenging but necessary step before these information can be plugged into InVEST or other decision-support tools. NatCap has created helper tools and documentation (or "on-ramps") to help InVEST users more easily prepare and format inputs for different models:
    1. InVEST 3.x Data Requirements - A sortable spreadsheet listing the required and optional inputs for each InVEST model
    2. InVEST 3.1.0 User's Guide - Every chapter includes a "Data Needs" section outlining input and formatting requirements for each model
    3. Marine InVEST Helper Tools - A GIS toolbox for pre- and post-processing data to be used with InVEST models (requires ArcGIS 10.x license)
    4. Experimental Software Tools - A discussion thread where NatCap staff and members of the broader community share details about new helper tools

     

      Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    Q: Where is the best place to find data?
    A: We have compiled a list of some global and regional MSP databases (see above). Environmental NGOs and local or regional government agencies often have data available upon request, if they don’t have an existing data portal. Be clear and transparent with partners about data needs and how your team intends to use this information. Some partners and collaborators are likely to have a network of potential data sources.

    Q: How do I handle lack of data?
    A: Lack of data can be a real problem. However, stakeholders often respond well to qualitative outputs too. Ultimately, it's important to be clear throughout the process as to where there was uncertainty or low confidence due to paucity of information.

    Q: How much is enough data?
    A: Revisit the project objectives and methodologies outlined during the scoping process. Start with just enough data to do preliminary analyses (e.g., run a selected tool). It is often easier to add and refine data as you go rather than waiting for "final" data.

    Barbados

    Data coordination between multiple partners was essential in Barbados. We used Google Drive to keep an updated list of the data we already had, the data we could readily access if needed, and the data we hoped to acquire. This online collaboration system helped us track data needs and acquisition (e.g., source, spatial coverage, resolution, etc.) which is difficult to accomplish with a remote team based in Barabados and the USA. Preliminary results from some InVEST models helped identify additional data needs and demonstrate for partners how data would be used.
