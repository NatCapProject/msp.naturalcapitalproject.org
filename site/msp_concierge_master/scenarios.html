Step 3: Scenarios (Alternative Futures)

  Overview

Scenarios are plausible representations of the future. Developing a set of alternative scenarios can be useful for examining how actions taken today may play out in the future. In the context of MSP, scenarios can be used to demonstrate how ecosystem services will change under different management and restoration alternatives and indicate the synergies and trade-offs among options. With many changes to consider when policies or mechanisms are designed, scenarios provide an important tool for planning and designing interventions.


 

  Goals

  • As part of a stakeholder-driven process, draft scenario storylines describing alternative pathways to some future date
  • Use scenario tools and stakeholder input to map differences in human use and marine management across these scenarios
  • Develop and finalize at least two future scenarios to compare and assess future outcomes (Analyze step)
 

  Tips

  • Scenarios reflected in InVEST are best expressed spatially (maps showing location of ocean uses, ecosystems, marine managed areas, etc.)
  • Alternative scenarios should be distinct (see pg 5 of our Primer for a complete list of effective scenario characteristics)
  • Explore our compilation of use-cases (below) to help find a scenario development tool that matches your project needs
  • Recognize that it is a time consuming process to adequately incorporate a diverse range of stakeholder visions and values

  Scenario Development Tools and Use-Cases

Below we list some popular scenario development tools. Click on the sub-bullets to learn more about how they were applied in MSP use-cases.

  1. InSEAM is a free online map annotation tool enabling multiple users to draw features on a base map. These drawings and annotations can be organized and downloaded as digital layers to faciliate the scenario development process.
  2. SeaSketch is a pay-for-service tool for ocean planners, stakeholders and the public that enables participatory marine spatial planning processes
    • North Pacific Coast - Marine Planning Partnership (MaPP) is integrating Marxan outputs in their SeaSketch project. MaPP is using the SeaSketch to draft protection management zones in four sub-regions, and discuss these with marine stakeholders.
    • Hauraki Gulf (New Zealand) - SeaSketch is being used by Stakeholder Working Group to create a marine spatial plan for the Hauraki Gulf Marine Park. This plan is being developed to ensure the long-term maintenance and enhancement of the Gulf’s cultural, environmental, economic, and social values.
  3. Habitat Priority Planner aids in making decisions about conservation, restoration, and planning by providing a means for obtaining critical habitat analyses that are consistent, repeatable, and transparent
  4. InVEST Scenario Generator provides a relatively simple method for generating scenarios based on land use change suitability
    • Vietnam - InVEST Scenario Generator was used to map changes in mangrove ecosystems under two future land use scenarios for the Ca Mau province of Vietnam.
  5. InVEST Habitat Risk Assessment (HRA) identifies where human activities create trade-offs among ES by posing risk that compromises habitat structure/function
    • Belize - InVEST HRA was used to assess the combined risk to habitats (coral reefs, mangrove forests and seagrass beds) from multiple ocean uses. These results were used to inform the design of the country’s first Integrated Coastal Zone Management (ICZM) Plan.
  6. Marxan with Zones facilitates design and configuration of protected area networks, to meet ecological, social, and economic objectives
    • St. Kitts and Nevis - Marxan with Zones generated a series of scenario-analysis maps and helped organize a wide range of information and assign actions to specific locations across the seascape. This tool was used to support the zoning decision-making process in St. Kitts and Nevis.
  7. IDRISI Land Change Modeler allows users to rapidly analyze land cover change and simulate future land change scenarios
  8. Coastal RIOS optimally sites habitat protection and restoration activities based on multiple planning objectives
    • West Florida - Coastal RIOS helped assess proposed actions following the Deepwater Horizon oil spill in the Gulf of Mexico, including the identification of best places to protect or restore seagrass, oyster, marsh, and dune habitats while balancing coastal protection, tourism and recreation, and fisheries objectives.
 

  Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How do I get started developing scenarios for MSP?
A: Scenario development is dependent on location, partners, and objectives. Refer to our list of scenarios development tools for ways to get started. For example, our Belize use-case (described below) used a habitat risk assessment tool combined with stakeholder engagement to draft and refine three alternative future scenarios.

Q: Can Marxan with Zones be used with InVEST models?
A: Marxan produces zoning configurations for the purpose of achieving multiple planning objectives. InVEST can use zoning maps produce by Marxan (e.g., optimal locations for different sectoral uses) to assess outcomes for people based on the future changes to ecosystem structure and function. NatCap has used various scenario tools as a means to create inputs for InVEST models. For example, the SLAMM model (Sea Level Affecting Marshes Model) was applied in the US Gulf of Mexico to produce maps showing where salt marshes migrate under different sea level rise scenarios for the year 2100.

Q: When are my scenarios final?
A: After preliminary analyses, scenarios can be iteratively refined to better reflect plausible alternative futures. Throughout the scenario development process, continue consulting stakeholders and local experts to ensure that your scenarios are plausible and will help your team produce information that can answer your specific research questions.

New England

During meetings with stakeholders and agencies working in the New England area, it became clear that the word "scenario" was not the preferred terminology to describe future ocean uses. NatCap continues work in the region to help provide decision makers with scientifically valid information to use for broader marine planning efforts. See our New England use-case for more details.

Belize

Scenarios reflecting three plausible futures for Belize in the year 2025 were developed through a succession of stakeholder meetings and workshops. These alternative scenarios are based on maps of the current distribution of ocean and coastal activities, existing and pending goverment plans, and stakeholders' values and preferences. The following set of rotating images describes this stakeholder-driven process where our partner, CZMAI, mapped three zoning configurations describing the location and extent of future human uses.



  • Maps of current human activities in the coastal zone were compiled by our partner, CZMAI
  • Two rounds of stakeholder consultations were held throughout the 9 coastal planning regions
  • Preliminary scenario storylines and InVEST outputs were presented at two workshops held in Belize City. Representatives from government, academia, environmental NGOs and civil society reviewed these early results and suggested improvements.
  • Final scenario descriptions and maps were used to assess future outcomes with InVEST
