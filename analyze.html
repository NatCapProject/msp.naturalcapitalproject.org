Step 4: Analyze (Assess Outcomes)

  Overview

The "Analyze" step involves assessing outcomes by modeling how change in ecosystems and social and economic conditions under alternative future scenarios leads to change in environmental benefits and values for people. Results can be spatially explicit and, when possible, should be directly tied to local beneficiaries (i.e., demand for those benefits) and reported using metrics that will resonate with stakeholders and policy-makers.


 

  Goals

  • Produce early outputs such as maps or summary tables to characterize the current distribution of ecosystem services
  • Through an iterative process (Iterate step), assess ES outcomes under plausible alternative future scenarios
 

  Tips

  • Before applying any decison-support tool make sure to understand its underlying assumptions and limitations
  • When deciding which tool(s) to use, examine existing use-cases where the tool has already been applied
  • Explore our compilation of NatCap MSP use-cases (below) to find a tool that suits your project needs
 

  MSP Decision-Support Tools and Use-Cases

Below we list some freely-available MSP analysis toolkits. Click on the tool's name to visit its website or click on the sub-bullet links to learn specifically how they were applied through an MSP use-case.

  1. AIRES
    • Madagascar - ARIES marine ecosystem services models also developed for Madagascar, focusing on coastal storm regulation and subsistence fisheries. Both of these services are critical for Madagascar, due to its reliance on fisheries for protein and its susceptibility to tropical storms forming in the southwest Indian Ocean.
  2. Atlantis
    • Northern Gulf of California (Mexico) - Atlantis was used to estimate the benefits of compliance with existing fisheries regulations in the Northern Gulf of California, including spatial management, seasonal closures, gear restrictions, removal of illegal fishing, buyouts and programs to encourage gear switching, and the combination of these regulations into ‘full enforcement’.
  3. Coastal Resilience
    • Gulf of Mexico - With its high incidence of storms and hurricanes and valuable ecological and economic resources, the Gulf region is a high-risk area with great potential to demonstrate natural risk reduction solutions. The Coastal Resilience approach and tools have been applied Gulf-wide and at specific sites, including those that help identify where to implement oyster reef restoration to meet social and ecological goals.
  4. Cumulative Impacts
    • California Current - Researchers applied methods developed to map cumulative impacts globally to the California Current using more comprehensive and higher-quality data for 25 human activities and 19 marine ecosystems.
  5. InVEST
    • Select InVEST MSP use-cases are described at the bottom of this page.
  6. MarineMap
    • California South Coast - MarineMap is an online mapping tool developed to assist in the design of marine protected areas (MPAs) in the Marine Life Protection Act Initiative process. The MarineMap project has recently been retired and developers are now working on SeaSketch.
  7. MIMES
    • Mississippi Delta - Wetlands, Hurricanes and the Economy: The Value of Restoring the Mississippi River Delta

  Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: How exactly can InVEST be applied to facilitate the MSP process?
A: See our use-case summaries at the bottom of this and other pages.

Q: If I have questions about using InVEST models or run into problems, where can I get help?
A: First, check the InVEST user’s guide chapter for the model you’re using. Each chapter covers the theory behind the model as well as detailed information about how data should be formatted and how to interpret the results. Each model's sample data provides an example of how input data should be formatted. Secondly, the NatCap User Help Forum allows users to post your questions. The forum is also a great way to interact with other InVEST users and NatCap staff.

Q: What guidance is available to help me choose from the different MSP decision-support tools?
A: In 2012 the Center for Ocean Solutions and PacMARA released a Decision Guide: Selecting decision support tools for marine spatial planning
